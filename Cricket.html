Cricket

Here are some of the rules of cricket:

  1. Teams: Two teams of 11 players compete.
  2. Field: The game is played on a large, oval or circular ground.
  3. Pitch: The pitch is a rectangular area in the middle of the field that's 22 yards long and 10 feet wide.
  4. Wickets: Two sets of three sticks, called wickets, are placed at each end of the pitch.
  5. Bails: Horizontal pieces called bails sit across the top of each wicket.
  6. Turns: Teams take turns at batting and bowling, which are called innings.
  7. Scoring: The batsmen try to score runs by hitting the ball and running to the other end of the pitch or hitting the ball over the boundary.
  8. Fielding: The fielding team tries to dismiss the batters by hitting the wickets, catching a shot, or LBW.
  9. Winning: The team with the most runs at the end of the game wins.
